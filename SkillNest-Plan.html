01

The concept

Tuition centers sell marks. SkillNest builds children: confidence, expression, language, creativity, fitness, and life skills, all inside the community they live in.

A tuition center

  • One subject, one goal: exam marks
  • Child travels to the teacher
  • Parent has no view of progress
  • Trust rests on one individual
  • Ends when the exam ends

SkillNest

  • Whole-child skill growth across academics, creative, sports, and life skills
  • Classes inside the society, walking distance, zero commute
  • Visible progress: reports, showcases, certificates
  • Trust rests on a verified community brand
  • A learning journey from age 4 to 16, class after class

One child can start with Kannada at 5, add Public Speaking at 8, Chess at 10, Coding at 12. That journey is the business.

03

What we start with

Two classes chosen to build trust, not maximize revenue. Both teachers are identified and ready to begin.

Public Speaking High parent aspiration, visible progress within weeks, kids can perform at society events.
Kannada Real need for non-Kannadiga families, school-syllabus adjacent, strong word of mouth potential.

Ages 4 to 16, residents only. Two batches per class: one weekday evening, one weekend. Free demo class for every child before enrollment.

The classroom

One room at home. 10 seats per batch. Small enough to feel personal, big enough to prove demand.

When batches fill past capacity, we add batches first, then move to the clubhouse classroom. Space scales only after demand forces it.

06

The revenue model

Marketplace economics: teachers earn most of the fee, SkillNest earns a commission for running everything around the teaching.

₹50,000 /month Illustration: 20 students × ₹2,500. Numbers below are hypotheses, not decisions.
₹35,000 · Teacher70% for teaching
₹15,000 · SkillNest30% for everything else

Price point

Test ₹1,500 / ₹2,000 / ₹2,500 anchors in demo-day conversations. Let parents tell us.

Teacher split

Start at 70/30. Zero acquisition cost for teachers may support 60/40.

Payment cadence

Monthly vs quarterly. Quarterly with a small discount improves retention and cash flow.

Discounts

Sibling and multi-class appetite: measure it before offering it.

Decision rule: pricing gets locked only after 20 paying enrollments and one full renewal cycle. Until then nothing is hardcoded, anywhere.

How revenue compounds later

Phase 1–2Class commissions · the core engine: monthly subscriptions across a growing class catalog
Phase 2–3Camps & workshops · summer camps, holiday workshops, birthday workshops: high-margin bursts on existing trust
Phase 3–4Events & competitions · inter-society competitions, showcases, leaderboards: engagement that markets itself
Phase 4+Kits & merchandise · learning kits, books, uniforms, merchandise attached to every enrollment
Phase 5+Platform & franchise · teacher marketplace fees, society dashboards, franchise licensing

Where software fits

Only after 20 paying students: a one-page site for credibility, then branding, then admissions flow, then portals. Rule: no portal gets built until the manual version of that workflow hurts. Building software before customers is the classic failure mode, and we are choosing not to fail that way.

10

The first 30 days

Everything below needs WhatsApp, Google Forms, a UPI QR code, and evenings. Nothing needs a developer.

WEEK1

Set up

  • Teacher agreements signed: split %, schedule, cancellation terms, one page each
  • Batch timings fixed: 2 per class, weekday evening + weekend
  • WhatsApp Business account for SkillNest
  • Google Form for demo bookings, UPI for payments
  • One poster + one WhatsApp creative (Canva)
WEEK2

Make noise

  • RWA blessing first. The single highest-leverage relationship in this plan
  • Post in society WhatsApp groups
  • Posters in lobbies, lifts, clubhouse notice board
  • Talk to 20 parents personally; write down objections verbatim
WEEK3

Demo days

  • Free demo sessions for both classes, max 10 kids per slot
  • Collect enrollment intent and pricing preference on the spot
  • Enroll on the spot with first-month payment via UPI
WEEK4

Classes begin

  • First paid batches start
  • Attendance + fees in one Google Sheet
  • Weekly parent update on WhatsApp with photos and what was covered. At this stage, this IS the product

Total build cost of this plan: roughly zero rupees and our evenings. The society is the distribution channel, and we already live in it.

11

How we'll know it worked

Phase 1 passes only when all five are true, within about 90 days.

  1. Paying students across the two classes20
  2. Month-2 renewal rate≥ 70%
  3. Unprompted parent referrals≥ 3
  4. Both teachers want to continue on the agreed splitYes
  5. Founder ops time per week≤ 10 hrs

If we miss: we revisit pricing, class selection, or the model itself before writing a single line of software. Missing the target is cheap now. It gets expensive the moment we build.
